## Event Guidelines

### Eligibility
- Open to college students from **all academic years and disciplines** across India.

### Team Composition
- Teams may have **up to 4 members**.
- Inter-college and interdisciplinary teams are allowed.

### Submission Policy
- **Daily submission limits** during the Build Phase.
- Encourages refinement, iteration, and quality-focused development.

### Offline Showcase
- Final prototype presentation will be conducted **offline on 20th February 2026**.
- **Physical presence is mandatory** to remain eligible.

### Certificates
- All participants will receive a **certificate of participation**.

### Plagiarism
- Copying code, models, or ideas without proper attribution is strictly prohibited.
- All submissions undergo automated originality checks.
- **Plagiarism results in immediate disqualification**.

### Judging
- All decisions made by the judging panel are **final and binding**.

### Late Submissions
- Late submissions will **not** be accepted under any circumstances.

### Dataset & Resources
- All required datasets and problem themes will be provided by the organizers during the event.

### Platform Compliance
- Teams must follow all rules provided on **Unstop** and any associated platforms.

### Email Consistency
- The email used for registration must remain consistent across all platforms.
- Multiple accounts are not permitted.

### Showcase Requirement
- Teams failing to attend the offline showcase on **20th February 2026** will be disqualified.

### Team Name Policy
- Team names must be professional.
- Offensive or inappropriate names may lead to disqualification.

## ðŸ“Š Data, Modeling & Reasoning Note

For each problem statement, a **curated dataset will be provided** to participants as a baseline.

Teams are expected to apply **Machine Learning techniques** for tasks such as prediction, forecasting, segmentation, classification, or pattern discovery, using appropriate evaluation metrics.

To support explanation, reasoning, summarization, and insight generation, teams may use **Generative AI**, including:
- Open-source large language models  
- Free-tier APIs such as **Gemini**

The choice of tools, frameworks, or architectures is left open.

Submissions should demonstrate **meaningful integration of ML + GenAI**, where analytical outputs are translated into **clear, human-understandable insights**.

> There is **no requirement** to build a chatbot or conversational interface.  
> GenAI may be used in any form that improves reasoning, clarity, or decision support.
